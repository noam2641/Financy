## ANTI-ANCHORING POSITION
The prior six-agent terminal verdict ("development should not begin") is **PARTIALLY REJECTED as over-broad**. Independent reading finds the spec's validation methodology unusually strong for a pre-code stage: it already mandates purged walk-forward with embargo (REQ-VAL-001/002), **date-grouped** cross-sectional IC as primary ranking metric with an explicit ban on pooled global Spearman (REQ-VAL-005), block-bootstrap CIs with serial-dependence adjustment (REQ-VAL-006), PBO/CSCV (REQ-VAL-008), and capacity + signal half-life + churn (REQ-VAL-009/010). These are the correct instruments and deserve credit. The defensible finding is **not** "do not begin" but "the alpha-existence go/no-go must be deferred behind a pre-registered Release-0 baseline study that adds five missing controls (DSR/trial registry, factor attribution, investable net-of-cost benchmark, effective-sample-size accounting, short-term-reversal orthogonalization)." Building the Truth Kernel (Release 0) to *test* alpha is appropriate; *asserting* alpha before those controls exist is not.

## FINDINGS

**F-A1-01 — Net-of-cost short-horizon cross-sectional edge is base-rate-unlikely; spec permits the claim but does not force the disproof.** LIKELY · BLOCKER (of alpha-existence claim, not R0 build) · REQ-LAB-001/005/006, REQ-VAL-004/009, R0-BL-006 · blocks release_0_definition, release_0_acceptance, final_holdout. Horizons 5/8/14 sessions (~1–3 weeks); target `ln(close[exit]/open[entry])`; acceptance = beat a *naive comparator* net of cost; no requirement pins the sustainable effect-size band a priori. External [A]: McLean & Pontiff 2016 (predictor returns −26% OOS, −58% post-pub); Chen & Velikov 2023 (net ≈ −1 bp/mo post-2005 original impl, ≈ +4 bp cost-minimizing); Novy-Marx & Velikov 2016 (<50%/mo turnover survives); Hou-Xue-Zhang 2020 (65% of 452 anomalies fail t>1.96 microcaps value-weighted out). Expected band: gross monthly IC ~0.02–0.04, net decile spread plausibly ≈ 0 without cost mitigation; half-life days-to-few-weeks; low capacity in the illiquid tail. Correction: pre-register a net-of-cost effect-size hurdle + investable benchmark; require `BLOCKED_NO_EDGE` unless the net band clears with DSR-adjusted significance. Simpler: restrict primary study to liquid large-cap / lower-turnover 14-session first. Verify: baseline report shows gross vs net decile spread and IC by liquidity quintile; net edge must not vanish in the top-2 liquidity quintiles.

**F-A1-02 — No factor attribution/neutralization; claimed "sector-excess alpha" not shown residual of market beta, size, value, momentum, low-vol, and short-term reversal.** CONFIRMED gap · BLOCKER · REQ-LAB-005/006, REQ-VAL-004/005 · blocks release_0_definition, release_0_acceptance, release_1A. Sector-excess ≠ factor-neutral; no requirement mandates a factor regression. External [A]: Avramov-Chordia-Goyal 2006 (short-horizon reversal profits concentrated in illiquid high-turnover names, smaller than costs); Jegadeesh 1990 (negative monthly serial correlation; bid-ask-bounce); Harvey-Liu-Zhu 2016 (residual-after-known-factors at t>3.0). Correction: mandatory factor-attribution regression on (Mkt-RF, SMB, HML, UMD, ST-Reversal, BAB/low-vol); promotion requires **residual** net-of-cost significance. Simpler: cross-sectionally orthogonalize to those factors ex ante and test whether any IC survives; if not, kill.

**F-A1-03 — Short-term-reversal & bid-ask-bounce contamination at the 5-session horizon not addressed.** LIKELY · HIGH · REQ-LAB-001/002. Entry-at-next-open (REQ-LAB-002) correctly avoids the close-to-next-open gap-capture leak (credit). But at ~1-week the residual signal is dominated by reversal; nothing requires a reversal control or spread-adjusted return. External [A]: Jegadeesh 1990; Avramov-Chordia-Goyal 2006; Della Corte & Kosowski. Correction: reversal-orthogonalized target variant; report IC before/after removing lagged-return exposure; consider dropping/de-weighting the 5-session horizon in MVP.

**F-A1-04 — "Naive comparator" under-specified; a long-only swing system needs an *investable* buy-and-hold SPY/sector-basket benchmark net of cost, beta-adjusted.** CONFIRMED gap · HIGH · REQ-VAL-004, R0-BL-006 · blocks release_0_definition, release_0_acceptance, paper, live. External [B/A]: Grinold & Kahn *Active Portfolio Management*; Chen & Velikov 2023. Because long-only, positive net return is trivially achievable by market beta in an up market. Correction: require BOTH (a) naive-forecast comparator (ranking skill) AND (b) investable benchmark = buy-and-hold SPY + matched sector-basket net of the same cost model, primary metric = benchmark-relative beta-adjusted return / information ratio.

**F-A1-05 — Effective (non-overlapping/regime-aware) sample size & IC-IR variance inflation from overlapping 5/8/14-session labels not required.** CONFIRMED gap · HIGH · REQ-VAL-005/006/001. Daily decisions with 14-session labels ⇒ ~14× temporal overlap; REQ-VAL-006 requires serial-dependence adjustment + block bootstrap (good) but no requirement states effective sample size or caps IC-IR t-stats for overlap-induced autocorrelation, nor ties embargo to max(label_horizon). External [B]: Boudoukh-Israel-Richardson 2018 "Long-Horizon Predictability: A Cautionary Tale"; overlapping-obs inference (Warwick/Neuberger). Correction: report effective sample size (Newey-West / non-overlapping-equivalent), IC autocorrelation, IC-IR CIs from block bootstrap with block length ≥ max horizon; embargo ≥ 14 + feature memory. Simpler: report non-overlapping-label IC-IR (sample every h sessions) as the conservative headline.

**F-A1-06 — Multiple-testing surface large but only PBO mandated; Deflated Sharpe Ratio + auditable trial registry should be mandatory.** CONFIRMED gap · BLOCKER · REQ-VAL-008; per-name horizon selection; REQ-LAB-011 stop/target grid; REQ-LAB-005 three target bases · blocks release_0_definition, release_0_acceptance, final_holdout. Search surface = 3 horizons × 3 target bases × N experts × barrier grid × per-name horizon selection; only PBO required (may be reported `PBO_NOT_ESTIMABLE`); DSR absent; no requirement to log the number of configurations tried. External [A/B]: Bailey & López de Prado 2014 (Deflated Sharpe); Harvey-Liu-Zhu 2016; Bailey et al. 2016 PBO. Correction: make DSR mandatory alongside PBO; require an immutable trial/experiment registry feeding N into DSR; count per-name horizon selection as a trial. Simpler: freeze one horizon + one target base for MVP to shrink N.

**F-A1-07 — Train/serve objective mismatch: model trained on the fixed-horizon research target but deployed under a barrier (stop/target) policy.** LIKELY · HIGH · REQ-LAB-001 vs REQ-LAB-011, REQ-LAB-003/004 (policy outcomes separated — partial credit) · blocks release_1A, paper. External [C]: López de Prado *AFML* (triple-barrier). Correction: train/rank on a barrier-consistent target when barriers active, OR prove empirically that fixed-horizon ranking is a sufficient statistic for barriered traded return; report `policy_return_h_if_filled` vs research-target divergence. Simpler: MVP with no stops (pure horizon exit) so research label = traded return; add barriers only as validated challenger.
